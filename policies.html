West TN Blazers

Purpose of Policies and Procedures

The purpose of these policies and procedures are to provide the directors, coaches, players, parents and everyone involved with the West Tennessee Blazers organization a set of procedures to operate by in growing and managing the Club. The club and all its participants are expected to abide by its directives. In addition, all involved are expected to abide by the procedures of our member organization, the Amateur Athletic Union (AAU). In the event of a potential contradiction with the Policies and Procedures contained herein, the Policies and Procedures of the organization mentioned above will take precedence. Such contradictions should be brought to the attention of the Directors, in writing, and remedied at the earliest possible opportunity.

Goal

The long-term goal of the club is to produce both quality players and people who can use basketball as a stepping-stone for improved high school play, college admission (and potentially basketball scholarships), and perhaps most importantly, personal development and enriching life experience. Winning will eventually come as a natural result of a well- executed process on behalf of the players, coaches and the club’s philosophy. The WTB philosophy will be accomplished by:

  • Putting the overall development of the player and the player’s team first while balancing the needs of the overall program.
  • Encouragement and support of the players by the Club, team and parents
  • Providing the best possible coaching and top-level training to facilitate players toward individual development and the team toward achieving its greatest potential.
  • Organizing the fairest and best possible teams through annual tryouts
  • Seeking out top-level competition with other teams of the same or higher level




Player Commitment and Expectations

An AAU team concept requires a much greater level of commitment than a recreational player or team. Each player, and parent, is expected to dedicate the time and effort necessary to continually improve his or her individual skills, contribute to the team’s success and do what is best for the team as a whole. Players that are not dedicated to becoming the best player possible may not be suited for the AAU team concept. It is completely understandable that some players may not wish to make such a commitment and may need to consider other options in fairness to those teammates that have made such a commitment. In addition to the above, each player is expected to complete the season or year in which they began and accepted a team’s invitation. If a player quits the team, for any reason, before the designated end of the season, that player will remain committed to all team fees and coaching expenses for the remainder of that season. This is to be considered a verbal commitment and enforceable by law.

Club and Team Structure

The ultimate decision for coach and/or assistant coach selection shall remain with the Directors. The Directors will consult with and inform the individual team manager(s) of the individual team(s). Any concerns, recommendations, criticisms, etc., of any coach will be done through the Directors, and not directly thru the coach. Assignment of Assistant Coaches will be done each season once the number of players on each team is confirmed. The Directors shall assess each team fees that support the administration of the Club, which may include coaching (if applicable) fees. These fees will be due and payable to the Club as determined by the Director’s and communicated thru the team managers. The coaching staff will be monitored by the directors, and recommendations for improvement will be made where indicated. The team coaches will report directly to the Directors, and not the parents or managers. Parental involvement and support are vital to the success of any team and the coach and team manager should encourage that involvement and support whenever possible. All decisions involving playing time, competition level and the scheduling of games and tournaments will be the ultimate responsibility of the Directors, however the team manager and coach shall have direct input. Concerns, protests, disagreements, etc., will be handled by the Director’s and decisions rendered and communicated back to the team manager(s) regarding the decision(s)in these cases. The manager may solicit input from the coach and give advice regarding participation in tournaments, but it is ultimately the decision of the team manager and Directors as to what is best for the Club and team(s). All decisions involving practice sessions will be made by the coach and team manager. Such decisions are not at the discretion of the parents, or players. Fees will be assessed to each team on a pro rata basis to cover the expenses of administrative costs, coaching fees, and other costs as determined by the Director’s.
