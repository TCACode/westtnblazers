Team Fundraising

In order to offset the majority of costs associated with growing and maintaining a top-level team, it is necessary for many teams to carry out fundraisers. The team manager may facilitate a number of fundraising events. Fundraising events could include general donations from the community. The greater the amount of fundraising, the less parents will be required to contribute as part of the team fees. All parents are expected to participate to the greatest extent possible. To comply with IRS regulations, any funds received through a team fundraiser must benefit all players equally. The WTB does not allow advertisements or sponsorships from tobacco, alcoholic beverage, adult entertainment or other companies that may serve as a bad example for participants. The WTB Directorâ€™s reserve the right to deny sponsorship or affiliation with the program or any team to any company or organization which they feels does not serve as a good example. Funds raised via fundraising are to be managed by the team manager and/or team treasurer. WTB may seek corporate sponsorships for the club as a whole. In order to avoid having multiple teams approaching the same potential corporate sponsors, individual teams should not seek corporate sponsors as a rule. If a team becomes aware of a potential corporate sponsor, that information should be shared with the Directors. Corporate sponsorships may be divided amongst the teams at the discretion of the Directors based on various criteria, but may not be equally divided amongst all teams.

Fundraisers will include:

  • selling Coca-Cola products
  • selling Butter Braids
  • Raffles
  • Babysitting
  • Community Yard Sales
